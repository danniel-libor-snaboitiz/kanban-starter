Architecture excerpt

Chose to persist Notification rows at write time rather than re-parsing at read time. Persisted rows let us track read_at per recipient, and mean the feed query is a single indexed lookup instead of a scan across every comment.

6. Reflection

The planning subagent broke the work into finer steps than I would have on my own, and that turned out to be the single most useful artifact I produced — each step was small enough to complete in one focused sitting. If I did this again I would start TDD from step 1 instead of easing into it around the mention parser; writing the failing test for the authorization policy retrofit was noticeably harder than writing it first would have been. The one honest gotcha was the drag-drop UI: I budgeted 60 minutes for step 8 and it took closer to 95, mostly because HTML5 drag events fire in a surprising order and I lost time reading the spec. Overall the workflow felt slower than jumping in would have, but the artifacts made the final report almost write itself.
