6Reflection

The biggest surprise was how much the two documentation subagents diverged in value: /init nailed the factual inventory but the real signal — the lowercase-enum trap, the ownership-traversal model, the “build a real notifications table” decision — only came from judgment layered on top. Deriving ownership by traversing Card → Column → Board rather than denormalizing a user_id onto every row kept the 403 guards single-sourced and made the authorization tests trivial to reason about. The one thing I’d change is to make the drag-and-drop a progressively-enhanced form from the start; building the async PATCH first and the no-JS <select> fallback second was backwards, since the fallback is what the feature test actually pins down. Overall, writing the failing test first genuinely changed the design — seeing the 403 test go red on a 302 made the missing guard obvious before a line of implementation.
